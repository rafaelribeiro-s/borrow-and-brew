# Borrow & Brew ☕
Educational project developed in Rust.

The goal is to progressively build the internal systems of the fictional coffee shop Borrow & Brew while learning the core concepts of the Rust programming language.

## Digitalizing a local coffee shop
Borrow & Brew is a small neighborhood coffee shop run by José, a barista passionate about specialty coffee.

For years, the business operated in a simple way:

* orders taken on paper,
* inventory tracking done in a notebook,
* payments recorded manually,
* and communication via phone and WhatsApp.

With the increase in the coffee shops's popularity, José hired two more people:

* Ana, responsible for customer service,
* João, who helps in the kitchen and with order delivery.

Now, the team faces several daily challenges:

* orders get lost during peak hours;
* customers receive the wrong orders;
* coffee and ingredient stock runs out without warning;
* there is no sales histroy;
* closing the cash register takes too long;
* and there is no organized table management.

José the decided to start digitalizing the business step by step.

Since the budget is tight, the coffee shop cannot afford expensive, ready-made systems. Instead, they decided to develop their own internal tools. To make this happen, José decided to hire a developer (you) to take care of building and implementing these systems.

The goal is to create small applications to help the coffee shop grow without losing the simplicity of local service.

## 👥 Team
### José — Owner and Barista

* Founder of the coffee shop.
* Knows a lot about coffee, but very little about technology.
* Wants something simple and easy to use.

### Ana — Customer Service

* Takes orders.
* Organizes tables.
* Records payments.

### João — Kitchen and Stock

* Prepares drinks.
* Controls ingredients.
* Updates inventory.

### You — Developer

* Responsible for designing and creating internal systems.
* Implements applications to solve daily operational issues (like order loss and inventory tracking)
* Develops custom, lightweight solutions using Rust.
